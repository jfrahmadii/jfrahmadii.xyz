About

Analytics that hold up to scrutiny

I'm a data analyst who likes the whole pipeline — not just the chart at the end. That means sourcing data from the original publisher, validating it against known figures, modelling it cleanly, and only then visualising it.

My Ontario decarbonization project is a good example: I built a production ETL against the IESO public feed, caught two real data bugs by reconciling against published totals, and documented exactly why the grid-connected numbers differ from province-wide figures. The goal is always work a stakeholder can trust and defend.
